<h2 id="hard-soft">Separate hard boundaries from soft signals</h2>

A relevance score can order candidates. It cannot buy permission, cross a sensitivity boundary, or displace required evidence. Context-budget design starts by separating `required`, `forbidden`, authorization, freshness, and hard resource limits from replaceable retrieval strategies.

<h2 id="loss">Make omission and loss visible</h2>

When optional material does not fit, the system can degrade. The result should still say what was omitted, which transform version was used, and where the conclusion applies. When a required item cannot be authorized or exceeds the hard budget, the default should fail closed rather than present an incomplete view as complete input.

<h2 id="questions">Questions to ask before implementation</h2>

- Does the ranker see body content before authorization?
- Do cache keys bind the actor, purpose, and revocation version?
- Can a summary still be traced to its source and declared loss?
- Do identical inputs and renderer versions produce stable output?

These questions turn “what went into the prompt” into a contract that can be reviewed at a system boundary.
